Organic Farming Knowledge Hub

Explore our comprehensive guides to mastering sustainable, chemical-free agriculture. From soil preparation to harvesting, we cover it all.

Basics

1. Mastering Soil Health & Fertility

Soil is the foundation of organic farming. Unlike traditional farming that uses synthetic NPK, we focus on building a living ecosystem.

  • Composting: Use green (nitrogen) and brown (carbon) materials in a 1:2 ratio.
  • Cover Cropping: Plant legumes to naturally fix nitrogen in the soil.
  • Crop Rotation: Never plant the same family in the same spot twice in a row.

Healthy soil contains billions of microorganisms that process nutrients for plants naturally.

Protection

2. Natural Pest Management (NPM)

In organic farming, we don't "kill" pests; we manage the ecosystem to keep them in check.

  • Neem Oil: A powerful natural repellent for over 200 species of insects.
  • Companion Planting: Plant Marigolds to repel nematodes and basil to deter flies.
  • Beneficial Insects: Encourage Ladybugs and Praying Mantises to live in your garden.
Advanced

3. Sustainable Irrigation & Water Conservation

Water is a precious resource. Organic farming significantly reduces water consumption by improving soil structure.

  • Mulching: Cover soil with organic matter to prevent evaporation.
  • Drip Irrigation: Delivering water directly to the roots reduces waste by 70%.
  • Rainwater Harvesting: Collect and store seasonal rain for year-round use.
Fertilizers

4. Making Your Own Organic Fertilizers

Stop buying expensive chemicals. Your farm produces everything you need.

  • Vermicompost: Use earthworms to turn food waste into "black gold."
  • Jeevamrutha: A powerful microbial culture made from cow dung and urine.
  • Liquid Seaweed: Excellent for providing trace minerals to leafy vegetables.
